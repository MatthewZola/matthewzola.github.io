Projects

Maxwell CubeSat

University of Colorado, Boulder

(August, 2017 - Present)

Maxwell is a 6U CubeSat being developed by the University of Colroado Boulder for the Air Force Research Laboratory's University Nanosatellite Program 9 competition. The satellite's mission is to demonstrate high rate communications utilizing CDMA technology and develop flight heritage for the MMA T-Dagher antenna built by MMA Technologies in Boulder, Colorado.

In the past semester, I helped redesign the entire satellite in order to interact with the MMA antenna and was responsible for creating many of the Solidworks CAD parts and assembly drawings.

Van's RV-12 Airplane

Teen Flight Program, Airway Science for Kids

(August, 2015 - June, 2017)

Teen Flight is a program where teenagers work alongside adult mentors to build a Van's RV-12 light-sport airplane from a kit of metal parts. This project demands a high level of precision in metalworking and part preparation skills, and an attention to detail to ensure the safety of the aircraft and its two passengers.

I was involved with the installation of the fuel lines, engine and avionics sub-sytems. By the end of the build, I developed a deep technical understanding of the plane and its various systems.
